The Concept
"I wanted the window to ask a question — not answer one. What do we see when we look through something rather than at it?"

Drawing on Lewis Carroll's threshold imagery and the Hermès atelier traditions of Lyon silk weaving, Ferenczi constructed a window that exists in perpetual becoming. Objects are not displayed — they are discovered. The silk floats as if just released from a hand. The leather bag casts a shadow that precedes it. The window is an invitation to look again.

Installed over eleven days at 24 Faubourg Saint-Honoré, the composition required 34 distinct silk samples, three artisans from the Bourgoin-Jallieu manufacture, and one mirror placed behind a mirror.

How to explore
Your complete guide
1
Position your phone
Hold your phone at arm's length, pointed at the window. The AR frame will align itself with the window's borders.
2
Watch for glowing points
Orange glowing dots will appear over objects in the window. These are interactive moments — each one is connected to a Hermès product in one the composition.
3
Tap to discover
Tap any glowing point to open its catalogue entry — material, artisan, origin, and the story of how this object came to live in this window at this moment.
4
Listen to the window
Audio plays automatically and shifts with proximity and time of day. At dawn, you may hear the atelier — scissors, thread, silk against silk. At dusk, maybe it is the distant sound of horses on cobblestone. The closer you are, the richer the audio becomes.
5
Take something with you
Visit the Story page to download wallpapers and animations as keepsakes — a fragment of the window, yours to keep long after the season ends.
